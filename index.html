Bigger Leaner Stronger 3-day split (push / pull / legs) on Mon·Wed·Fri, combat and yoga on the in-between days to keep the joints happy. Morning HIIT + journal stays your daily anchor — keep it light on lift days or use it as a warm-up.

📋 The lifts (barbell · dumbbells ≤15kg · KB · bands)

Barbell lifts go heavy, 4–6 reps. Dumbbell/KB/band lifts go higher-rep to near-failure — your 15s cap the load, so you progress with reps, tempo, bands and shorter rest instead.

Push (Mon) — chest, shoulders, triceps

Barbell Overhead Press (rack) — 3 × 4–6 heavy
Incline Dumbbell Press (mini bench) — 3 × 8–12
Weighted / banded Push-up — 3 × to near failure
Dumbbell Side Lateral Raise — 3 × 12–15
Overhead DB/KB Triceps Extension — 3 × 10–15

Pull (Wed) — back & biceps

Barbell Deadlift — 3 × 4–6 heavy
Barbell Bent-Over Row — 3 × 6–8 heavy
One-Arm Dumbbell Row — 3 × 8–12
Band Lat Pulldown (anchor band high) — 3 × 12–15
Barbell Curl — 3 × 6–8 · DB Hammer Curl — 3 × 10–12

Legs (Fri) — squat-focused

Barbell Back Squat (rack) — 3 × 4–6 heavy
Barbell Romanian Deadlift — 3 × 6–8 heavy
DB/KB Bulgarian Split Squat (rear foot on bench) — 3 × 8–12
KB Goblet Squat or Walking DB Lunge — 3 × 10–15
KB Swings (finisher) — 3 × 15 · Calf Raise — 3 × 15–20
⚙️ How to run it (BLS method)

Barbell lifts: 3 hard sets of 4–6 reps (~80–85% 1RM), rest 2–4 min, stop 1–3 reps shy of failure. Double progression: hit 6 on all sets → add ~2.5 kg next time.

Dumbbell / KB / band lifts (capped at 15 kg): when the weight gets easy you can't just add a plate, so make it harder another way — more reps (up to ~15), slower 3–4 sec lowering, a band over the dumbbell, a pause at the bottom, or shorter rest. Take these close to failure.

Warm up each first barbell lift with a couple of light sets (~50%, ~70%) before your working weight.

Nearly-50 rule: heavy means form first, ego never. Grumbly joint → swap the move or drop the weight. Combat and yoga days are there so you don't break — take them. Run a lighter deload week if anything feels beaten up.

🍗 Protein & eating

Protein target ≈ 150 g/day. This is the lever that keeps muscle while the fat comes off — non-negotiable on a deficit at our age. Roughly 30–40 g per meal across your eating window.

Easy hits: eggs, Greek yoghurt/skyr, chicken, white fish, prawns, lean beef, tofu/edamame, cottage cheese, a whey shake to top up.

Modest deficit, not starvation. Protein + veg + smart carbs around training. The tummy goes when the whole-body deficit runs — there's no spot-reduction, but the deficit + steps + HIIT + sleep is exactly what shifts belly fat.

Hydrate hard (helps appetite and the fast), and add a pinch of salt / electrolytes on HIIT mornings.

⏳ 16:8 fasting

Eat in an 8-hour window — e.g. 12:00–20:00. Black coffee, tea, water in the morning. Do your AM HIIT fasted, eat after.

You enjoy the challenge of it — lean into that. If a day's training is heavy or sleep was poor, it's fine to widen the window. Sane beats strict.

🚫🍷 Alcohol & mood

Zero for the 5 weeks. This single change will do more than any workout: better sleep, lower resting HR, faster fat loss, steadier mood — and it directly unwinds the loop you described.

The streak counter up top is your friend. Protect it. Have a plan for the wobbly evening: alcohol-free beer, a strong tea, a walk, journal, bed.

This sits alongside, not instead of, the support you've already got. Movement, sleep and no booze are powerful for low mood — but keep your therapist/GP in the loop, and be as kind to yourself on the hard days as you'd be to a mate.

😴 Sleep & steps

Sleep is your one number with real upside. With an 18-month-old, perfect sleep isn't on the table, so this is "prioritise it when you can", not another box to fail. Aim 7h+ when the night allows. Cutting alcohol alone will lift the quality of whatever sleep you get. Wind-down: screens down 30 min before bed, room cool and dark.

10k+ steps daily — you already smash this. It's the quiet engine of fat loss. Keep it up.

Keep it sane. This is meant to make you feel strong and clear, not to become another stick to beat yourself with. A missed box or a heavier weigh-in isn't failure — it's data. Don't chase the deficit with punishment. If any of it starts feeling like overwhelm rather than momentum, ease off and tell someone. Hitting your target by the 20th would be brilliant; getting close and feeling like yourself again is already a win worth celebrating.
